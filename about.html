History of Alabat

Alabat, officially the Municipality of Alabat, is a peaceful island town known for its hospitality and rich natural resources.

The town is home to a few speakers of the critically endangered Inagta Alabat language, listed by UNESCO.

Founded in 1882 by a Spanish priest, the town originally built an ermita that honours Our Lady of Monte Carmelo.

Its first name was Gordo, meaning "fat", due to the island's shape. It was later changed to Gordon, Barcelona, and finally Alabat in 1900.

Etymology

The name Alabat came from the word Alâbât (local Tagalog word for balustrade or balcony).

Another folklore says the name was from Muslim origins Allah-bat, which means Bathala (God).
